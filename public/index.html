The Problems We Solve

Communities face interconnected water, waste, and resource challenges that demand integrated solutions.

Greywater Waste

70% of household wastewater (from showers, sinks, laundry) is discarded untreated, wasting recoverable water.

Critical Impact

Freshwater Scarcity

Rising costs and climate stress make potable water increasingly scarce. Communities need alternatives.

Growing Crisis

Improper Waste Disposal

Lack of sorting infrastructure leads to contamination and lost recycling opportunities.

Systemic Issue

Poor Environmental Visibility

Communities lack real-time data on water usage, waste generation, and resource efficiency.

Data Gap

Non-Revenue Water (NRW) Losses

Utilities lose 20-30% of water to leaks and theft, costing millions annually.

Behavioral Disconnect

Our Commitment

Ethics, Transparency & Responsible AI

We believe AI must serve communities, not surveil them. Our platform is built on principles of transparency, privacy, and local control.

Transparent Algorithms

All AI models are explainable. Communities can see how decisions are made, from leak detection thresholds to behavioral recommendations.

Privacy-First Design

No personally identifiable data is collected without consent. Aggregate metrics only. Users control their data and can opt out anytime.

Community Governance

Local councils have final say on system rules, data usage, and feature priorities. The platform adapts to community values, not vice versa.

Open Data Standards

Environmental metrics are published openly for researchers, NGOs, and policymakers. No data silos, no vendor lock-in.
